Help & About

The longest-running unofficial party list for Black Hat, BSides Las Vegas, and DEF CON in Las Vegas. Plan your Hacker Summer Camp week, track applications, and spot new listings as vendors publish them.

All times are Pacific (PT). Signup links open the vendor's page — we do not run registrations here.

What's new

  • Feature tour — first-time visitors get a guided walkthrough; replay anytime with the button at the top of Help.
  • New badge — parties added since your last visit.
  • Updated badge (amber) — title, time, location, or description changed.
  • Party reminders — in event details, pick 15 min, 30 min, or 1 hour before start, or Off. Personal alerts only; not the site-wide Subscribe push.
  • Mark all seen — clears New/Updated when you are caught up.
  • Smarter refresh — every 6 hours Aug 1–11; 48 hours off-season. Refresh Now forces a sync (5-minute cooldown).

Views & badges

  • Card view — grid of events; click for details and signup.
  • Agenda view — events by day with time ranges.
  • New / Updated — shown on cards, agenda rows, and the event modal.
  • Show New / Updated filter — focus on unseen or freshly synced parties.

Your schedule

  • Approved (green) — plan to attend; counts toward Party-O-Meter™.
  • Pending (blue) — applied, waiting to hear back.
  • Rejected (red) — ruled out; hide with Hide Rejected.
  • Saved automatically in this browser — no account required.

Getting invited

  • Use a work email — vendors often prioritize corporate addresses over free providers (Gmail, Yahoo, Outlook.com, etc.). A company domain signals you are industry, not a random signup.
  • Fill forms accurately — title, company, and LinkedIn matter on many invite-only lists. Use Cut & Paste to keep your details consistent across signups.
  • Vendors will contact you — when you submit a registration, the host gets your info and may email you about approval, waitlists, or event updates. Use an inbox you actually check during Drunk Camp.
  • Expect follow-up — approval is not instant on most parties. Mark events Pending here so you remember what you applied for while you wait.

Find & filter

  • Search — title, location, date, or description. Prefix with - to exclude temporarily (e.g. -microsoft). Click Always hide to save an exclusion permanently; remove saved words with the × chips below search.
  • My Approved / Pending / Friends — focus on your list or imported friend schedules.
  • Status filters — below search in Card or Agenda view: Hide Past, Hide Rejected, Hide Approved, Show Pending Only, Show My Approved, Friends, New/Updated, etc.
  • Agenda day filters — weekday checkboxes below status filters, Agenda view only.
  • Keyword filters — collapsible panel above the view tabs for bulk edit; same list as the removable chips below search.

Staying current

  • Last refreshed — timestamp and auto-refresh interval above the list.
  • New / updated counts — e.g. 2 new · 1 updated; use Mark all seen when done.
  • Refresh Now — pull the latest feed before planning your day.
  • First visit — existing parties are not marked New; only additions after your baseline.
  • Subscribe tab — optional site-wide push alerts when the DCP team sends an update. Not the same as per-party reminders.

Share & tools

  • Share a party — link icon in event details, or hold a card/agenda row (mobile) / right-click (desktop). Opens that party for anyone with the link, even if your filters would hide it.
  • Export / import — Share/Sync encodes Approved, Pending, and Rejected choices.
  • Friends — import a nickname + code; friend approvals show in orange.
  • Flex on 'em — share your approved count from Party-O-Meter™.
  • Cut & Paste — store name, email, title, shirt size, etc. for fast signup forms.
  • Submit a party — Submit tab or paste a URL in search.
  • Dark mode — sun/moon toggle in the header.

Privacy

  • Schedules, filters, profiles, and "seen" baselines stay in your browser only.
  • Share codes are encoded, not encrypted — share only with people you trust.
  • Sign Up, Approved, and Rejected clicks are tracked anonymously for popularity stats.
